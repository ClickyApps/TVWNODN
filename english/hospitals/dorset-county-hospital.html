Visiting information

Parents
There is open visiting for parents

Number of visitors
Number of visitors is restricted to three per cot; one of those visitors must be the Baby’s parent.

Siblings
Siblings are welcome to visit with their parent from 4pm to 6pm

Restrictions
We kindly ask that visits refrain from visiting the Neonatal Unit if they are unwell.

We strongly recommend no visitors enter the Neonatal Unit if they have been working in another clinical area of the Hospital or a Nursing/residential Home.

No visitors under the age of 16 are permitted to visit unless they are a sibling.

Grandparents
Grandparents are welcome to visit with a parent from 4pm to 6pm.

Hygiene and infection prevention
Please refrain from visiting if you are unwell.

We kindly ask that you wash your hands upon entry to the Neonatal Unit and decontaminate with hand sanitizer.

Family and friends
The visiting of family and friends is between 4pm to 6pm.

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
There are four residential rooms for parents to use in preparation for taking their babies home.

Two of those rooms have ensuite facilities.

Parent facilities
Each bedroom has a double bed and soft furnishings.
They also have a flat screen TV with a built in DVD player.
There is a kitchen/breakfast room area for the use of resident and non-resident parents.
The kitchen is equipment with, fridge freezer, microwave, cutlery, crockery and glassware.

Parent support group
We are in the process of setting up a parent support group.

Facilities for siblings
There is a family room equipped with items to encourage imaginative play.
There is also a Flat Screen TV and Portable DVD players with a selection of DVDs.
Children under the age of 10 years must be supervised by an adult.

Mobile phone use
Mobile phones can be used on the Neonatal Unit, but we kindly request that the use of them does not infringe upon the privacy of babies, siblings, other parents, visitors and staff.

Bereavement support
There is a designated Neonatal Nurse responsible for coordinating the care and support for bereaved parents.

There is also support available from the Chaplaincy Service.

Telephone and computer access
There is a telephone available in each bedroom which accepts incoming calls only.

Guest Wi-Fi is available upon request.

Counselling
Counselling can be arranged via BLISS.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
All staff on the Neonatal Unit has been trained to offer and provide up to date breast feeding support.

There is a designated Breast Feeding Neonatal Nurse.

The Neonatal Unit alongside the Maternity Unit has been accredited with the UNICEF Baby Friendly Initiative. There is a comfortable private room where Mothers can express for their baby. We can also provide screens so that Mothers can sit alongside their babies to express.

Uninterrupted skin to skin care is actively encouraged.

Who's who on the unit?

Clinical Lead
The Clinical lead for the Neonatal Unit is Dr A. Deketelaere, Consultant Paediatrician

Play specialists

There are no services available on the Neonatal Unit.

Family centred care/developmental care lead
There is a designated Neonatal Nurse for Family Centred care and Developmental care.

There is also a Children’s Therapist, who visits the Neonatal Unit weekly to assess the development of the pre-term babies and is available during drop in sessions to provide long term advice to parents.

We also perform with consent from parents the filming of movements of a certain group of babies. This is then interpreted by Catha Kopecky who is trained to interpret the movements using the Prechtl method of analysis.

Nurse manager
The Manager for the Neonatal Unit is MS Alison Bryan who also works two clinical shifts per week.

Other important stuff
We have two Advanced Neonatal Nurse Practitioners who have undertaken additional training and have considerable experience to provide care and treatment to babies on the Neonatal Unit.

Breastfeeding advisor
All staff on the Neonatal Unit is trained to provide up to date advice to those Mothers wishing to breast feed.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The Neonatal Unit is a secure area.

Parents and visitors are requested to use the intercom system to say who they are and who they have come to visit.

A member of staff then decides whether they are have permission to visit and unlocks the door for them to gain access.

There is also a security camera at the entrance to the Neonatal Unit with a display screen for staff to view inside the Neonatal Unit.

Talking to the medical team
Please let a member of the Nursing staff know if you would like to talk to a member of the Medical Team.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The member of staff caring for your baby or the Nurse in Charge of the Shift

Ward round
Daily Ward round from 09.30 approx.
Not all babies admitted to the Neonatal Unit are seen on the daily ward round so please check with the Nurse looking after your baby.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Any member of the Neonatal Team or a member of PALS.

Baby rest time
We have a “Quiet Time” from 2pm to 4pm.
