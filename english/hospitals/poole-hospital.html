Visiting information

Parents
Unrestricted visiting for parents with the exception of Thursday mornings

Number of visitors
3 at the cotside including parents

Siblings
Siblings are permitted to visit with their parents. If there is evidence of increased levels of infections in, for example schools, visiting for siblings may be restricted or suspended but this is rare.

Restrictions
None

Grandparents
With parents’ permission are able to visit if accompanied by a parent.

Hygiene and infection prevention
Please use the hand gel available on entering the unit. You will be asked to remove your coats.
Please do not visit the unit if you have a cough, cold, fever or stomach upset.
If you are suffering from diarrhoea and vomiting you should be symptom free for 48 hours before you visit the unit

Family and friends
Visiting time is from 13:30-15:00 or 18:00-19:00

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
Enter the maternity unit on St Mary’s Road. Turn left and walk to Central Delivery Suite where there is a stair case. The Neonatal Unit is on the 1st floor opposite the stairs and lift

Talking to the medical team
A consultant will be on duty each day who will update you if you are present on the ward round. The nurse caring for your baby can also arrange for your babies consultant to discuss care with you

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
You can talk to the nurse caring for your baby, the advanced neonatal nurse practitioner or your baby’s consultant

Ward round
09:00-11:00

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
You can talk to your baby’s consultant, the nurse in charge of the shift, or matron

15:00-17:00
