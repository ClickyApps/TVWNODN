Religious and spiritual support
The chaplaincy team are available 24/7 to offer pastoral support. They have contacts for all religions and faith traditions with any specific needs. They can be contacted on 01865 857921. There is a chapel and prayer room on the grounds of the Horton hospital which can be accessed 24/7.

Catering/refreshment facilities
The SCBU provides tea and coffee making facilities for parents to make a hot drink. These are to be consumed in the parent’s snug area on the unit. Cold drinks may be consumed in the clinical areas adjacent to your baby. The SCBU environment is very warm and parents can become dehydrated, so a water fountain is provided in the SCBU.

Food may not be consumed on the unit but can be eaten in the parents’ room. There is a fridge,toaster and microwave in the room. Please ask staff for access. Meals are provided for all mothers of babies on SCBU, throughout their entire stay. These are available on the adjacent maternity ward. Breakfast 8-8.30, lunch 12-12.30, supper 1730-1830, the maternity housekeeper will come and find you and offer you a meal.

There are vending machines in the reception area of the maternity unit, with drinks, sandwiches and confectionary.

The hospital dining room is in the main hospital and serves hot meals. It is open from 0730-1600 every day, servicing breakfast until 11.30 and lunch between 12-14.30 Monday-fri.

The league of friends shop in the main hospital and is open mon-fri 10-4 and 3-5 at the weekend. They sell drinks and confectionary.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
The Oxford University Hospitals NHS Trust (OUH) Patient Advice and Liaison Service (PALS) is committed to improving the service we give to our patients. It is a confidential service that aims to:

  • advise and support patients, their families, and their carers
  • listen to patients' concerns, queries and suggestions
  • help sort out problems quickly on your behalf
  • Inform patients, their families and their carers about the Trust's Complaints Procedure
  • Assist you if you have any concerns about your rights as a patient and how the OUH is fulfilling its part of the NHS Constitution with regard to your own care.

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
We have one room for parents to stay in the hospital overnight. This room is generally used for parents to stay and care for their baby just before the baby goes home, or for parents who need to stay close by so that can establish breast feeding.
Priority goes first to parents of any baby who is critically unwell.
The nurses on the unit will happily discuss your needs for accommodation,

Parent facilities
We have a number of facilities available to the parents on the unit; we have a parent’s room and snug area which can be used for a rest or time away from the unit. Food and drinks are available from a variety of different places, and the staff are happy to help and provide support as necessary.

Parent support group
For a chance to speak to other parents in the same situation and for support and guidance, We offer the bliss family support helpline on 0500618140 mon – Fri 9am-9pm.

Facilities for siblings
We have a small snug area for the families of SCBU patients, this area can be used by the siblings and families. There is a TV/DVD system, and some toys which can be used when visiting the unit.

Mobile phone use
You are welcome to take photographs of your baby using a camera or mobile phone, staff will be happy to help you get some precious moments.
There is a low but possible risk that a mobile phone signal could affect some of the specialist equipment used to care for the babies on the neonatal unit. Because of this and also to keep noise levels down visitors are asked not to use their phones whilst in the nurseries. However calls can be made and taken in the parent’s room, or in the corridors outside the unit.

Bereavement support
The Bereavement Service offers practical and emotional support to the immediate family or next of kin of anyone who dies as a patient of the Trust, together with anyone who is bereaved and connected with the Trust.
This includes working with medical, midwifery and nursing colleagues to ensure prompt completion of all the necessary paperwork after a death, as well as explaining to the bereaved how to register a death and how to find a funeral director, or arrange a funeral without a funeral director.
We work closely with the Coroner's Service, the Oxfordshire Registration Service and local funeral directors to ensure that the bereaved have access to all the information and services they require. When necessary the bereavement team will refer families to the appropriate Coroner's Officer.

Other important stuff
The Horton SCBU is connected with 5 other neonatal unit (Milton Keynes, oxford, Aylesbury, reading and slough) known jointly as the ‘Thames valley neonatal network’. We aim to care for all the babies from our area requiring neonatal care, however not all babies need the same amount of care. Within the network Banbury is a level one unit, caring for sick babies and babies greater than 34 weeks gestation. Milton Keynes, Aylesbury, reading and slough are level 2 units, providing some breathing support and more specialised care for early and sick babies. Oxford is a level 3 unit and gives intensive care, including caring for babies with specialised problems requiring surgery and or cardiology input.

All the staff in each neonatal unit are qualified and experienced in giving the level of care that unit provides so you can be confident of the best care for your baby at whichever level of nursery your baby is being cared for.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The special care Baby Unit is based in the maternity unit, off Hightown road, at the Horton general hospital, ox16 9al.
The maternity unit is a 2 floor white building to the east of the hospital, situated off Hightown road, The special care baby unit is situated on the first floor which can be reached by the stairs directly to the right of reception or by the lift which is to the right of the staircase. Between 10pm-6am the external sliding doors are locked but you can gain entry by using the intercom system to the right of the door.

Talking to the medical team
The doctors and nurses caring for your baby want you to be involved and informed in every aspect of your baby’s care and time on the unit. We will talk to you whenever you come to visit your baby and update you about their care and progress. If we need to contact you when you are not visiting, then we will contact you by phone, please ensure you have given us your correct and most current contact details.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
Each day there will be a nurse allocated to care for your baby. There are always doctors on the unit who you can ask any questions to.

Ward round
There is a daily ward round which is led by the consultant and doctors, during this they will discuss your baby’s progress and any changes that will be made to the care of your baby. You are welcome to be on the unit when this is happening.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
You are welcome to approach any member of the SCBU staff team to discuss your concerns. If you feel it has not been dealt with in an appropriate manner this can be escalated as necessary.
