Religious and spiritual support
The chaplaincy office is situated adjacent to St Mary’s Hospital Chapel on level A in the main building, next to the lifts. The Multi Faith quiet room is also located here. The chaplaincy service has contact with other faith leaders on the Island and can provide information on how to get in touch with them if required. There is a worship service every Sunday morning at 10.30. Church of England, Roman Catholic and Free Church chaplains available and also available via bleep in an emergency.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
If you wish to make a formal complaint, in the first instance it may be helpful to contact the Patient Experience Officers (PEO’s) in the Complaints/PALS team who will be able to offer initial advice and support and guide you through the complaints process. Alternatively you may wish to write directly to the Chief Executive. Patient Experience Officers contact details are:

  • Office Hours: 0900hrs – 1630hrs Monday to Friday, excluding bank holidays
  • Direct Tel: 01983 534850 or 01983 534420 or via the hospital switchboard 01983 524081
  • Email: quality@iow.nhs.uk or PALS@iow.nhs.uk

Interpreting services
The ISLE OF WIGHT NHS TRUST has a contract with Language Line Interpreting Service, who provide interpreting by telephone in 145 languages, 24 hours a day seven days a week. Connection to a trained interpreter takes around 25 seconds without booking, and the service is accessed via 0845 310 9900 (option 1) or 0800 169 2879 (option 1). We also have volunteer interpreters for Polish, Russian, Bulgarian and Czech available.

Other shops on site/nearby
There are two shops open in the main reception area at St. Mary's Hospital for light refreshments, newspapers etc. The shops are run by the Friends of St. Mary's. They also offer a trolley service to the wards where you can purchase drinks, snacks, toiletries, newspapers and other useful things such as greetings cards. Opening times: 7:00am until 4:00pm, weekdays only.

Sainsburys supermarket and petrol station located within 1 mile of the hospital

Visiting information

Parents
Open visiting

Number of visitors
2 visitors to a cot

Siblings
Accompanied by parents, as long as they are well.

Restrictions
No visiting if unwell. Children under 16 not allowed if they are not siblings. Visiting restricted to parents only if the whole hospital has visiting restrictions due norovirus etc.

Grandparents
Open visiting with Parents permission

Hygiene and infection prevention
Hand washing on entry to unit and prior to touching baby. Outside coats to be hung on hooks provided. Please do not visit if unwell and for 48hours after any diarrhoea and vomiting.

Family and friends
4 additional named visitors allowed, to be decided upon admission.
