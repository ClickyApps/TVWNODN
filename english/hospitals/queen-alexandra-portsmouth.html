Visiting information

Parents
Welcome all day

The following times are staff handover and we ask that where possible you can support us by not visiting / phoning during these times. Please feel free to use the parent facilities during these times:

  • 07:15 - 08:00
  • 19:15 - 20:00

Number of visitors
2 per cot including 1 parent. There may be times when the staff request that visitors leave this is to ensure patient care or safety is not compromised.

Siblings
Brothers and sisters are permitted to visit. Not after 8pm

Restrictions
Baby rest time parent visiting to be agreed with the nurse helping you look after your baby

Grandparents
Must be accompanied by a parent

Hygiene and infection prevention
Before entering the NICU, please gel your hands. Germs carried on the hands are harmful to premature and sick babies. Please wash and gel your hands, when requested to do so.

All visitors to the NICU are requested to leave outdoor clothing and bags in lockers. Please collect a key from reception desk.

If you are suffering or recovering from any infectious illnesses such as diarrhoea, vomiting, coughs, colds or flu we would ask you not to visit until you have properly recovered.

Family and friends
Short visits only and must be accompanied by a parent. We regret no children are permitted to visit the unit.

Other important stuff
Wheelchairs
For those who need wheelchairs they are available at various locations around the hospital including the main and north entrances and in the multi-story car park. Patients have to insert a pound coin to release the wheelchair from a 'train' of other wheelchairs.

You will be able to reclaim that pound coin when you return the wheelchair to the 'train', thus there is no charge to use them.

Breastfeeding advisor
All the staff are trained to support feeding and members of the Family Support Team led by Carol Buxton are available for extra support. You can recognise them by their lilac uniforms.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The NICU (B9) can be found on ‘B’ level. There is a security door entry system. Please press the bell, and wait patiently for the ward clerk or nurse to answer. They will ask your name, check that your baby is on the NICU and let you in. For the security of all babies, do not let anyone else enter the NICU with you.

Talking to the medical team
Doctors are available 24 hours a day. If you wish to speak to your baby’s Consultant just ask the nurse helping you to care for your baby.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
Please ask the nurse helping you to care for your baby

Ward round
Ward round is usually between 09.30-12.00 depending on numbers of babies

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
The Nurse in charge of the shift she wears a big RED badge and is easily identifiable or you can contact Carol Moore Matron whose office is based in the entrance corridor on the NICU

Baby rest time
13.30-14.30 where we reduce noise and lighting levels
