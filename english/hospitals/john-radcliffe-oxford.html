Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
If you feel that the issue has not been resolved, PALS is an additional service that works with patients, relatives and carers to solve areas of concern that affect any aspects of services provided in the hospital.

PALS is impartial and confidential and can:

  • Listen to your concerns, suggestions or queries.
  • Liaise with hospital staff on your behalf.
  • Help the hospital learn from patient experiences.
  • Put you in touch with other agencies who can help.
  • Guide you through the formal complaints process if necessary.

Hygiene and Infection prevention
Please wash your hands and hang up outside coats on entering the unit. Lockers are available for valuables. Please wash your hands prior to touching your baby. Hand washing is very important to protect your baby and the other babies on the unit. It is imperative that you use the hand gel located by the entrance of the nurseries when entering and leaving the unit. All outdoor clothing must be removed and placed on the coat hooks provided. Coin operated lockers are available at the nurseries entrance. They are operated using a £1 coin which is given back when you return the key. We also ask you to remove all jewellery including your watch since they carry a lot of bacteria and are difficult to wash. You also need to roll your sleeves to the elbows and then thoroughly wash your hands, wrists and forearms. After, reapply hand gel. Remember to always wash your hands before and after touching your baby. Food is not permitted inside the nurseries but can be eaten in the parent’s room. Hot drinks are allowed but extra care must be taken to prevent accidents. Therefore, they should be kept away from the cot and baby.

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
We have 3 rooming in rooms on the unit, usually for use to establish feeding before going home. A self contained flat just outside the unit and 2 houses, close to the hospital, with 3 and 4 bedrooms, sharing facilities including a washing machine. There is no charge for these.
They are allocated by the Shift coordinator on a priority/need basis and for limited periods. Children are not allowed to stay.

Parent facilities
We provide a sitting room for the parents whose babies are on the Unit. There are sofas, a television, a microwave as well as tea/coffee making facilities. Please make use of this room at any time during the day. Ask the nurse looking after your baby to show you where the room is located.
There is a visitor’s toilet near the LDU. Ask a member of staff to show you where it is.

Parent support group
SSNAP is the charity that supports the Newborn Care Unit at the OUH Trust. It is based on level 2 near the Women’s Centre entrance. The charity was set up in 1982 at the hospital and since then, it has raised millions of pounds to help sick and premature babies with a wide range of medical needs.

They provide support through the funding of two family support workers who offer emotional and practical support on the unit day to day. They are also trained at assessing families who will need additional help in caring for their baby.

They coordinate a Parent Help Volunteer group who visit parents and families with a baby in the NCU. All the volunteers have had an experience in the NCU and they offer a listening ear. They make visits to the maternity wards and the NCU on Monday evenings and Thursday mornings.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
The Newborn Care Unit has registered intent to go “Baby Friendly”. The UNICEF UK Baby Friendly Initiative is the first ever national intervention to have a positive effect on breastfeeding rates in the UK. The overall aim is to ensure that all parents make informed choices about feeding their babies and are supported in their chosen feeding method.
On the NCU, we have a dedicated infant feeding team to support you with expressing and breastfeeding. All the nursing staff are trained in breastfeeding and lactation management and are there to support and guide you.
There are breast pumps to use next to your baby, or you can use a private expressing room.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The Newborn Care Unit(NCU) is located in the Women’s Centre. The entrance is on level 2 and the unit is on the same level. From the entrance, go straight and follow the signs to the NCU. There are signs for the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), High Dependency Unit (HDU) and Low Dependency Unit (LDU). You need to press the intercom to gain entry.

Talking to the medical team
Members of the medical team will be available to discuss your baby’s care throughout your stay.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The Nurse allocated to care for your baby, the Doctors and the Consultant.

Ward round
09:00-11:00 in all areas, 16:30-17:00 in High dependency, 17:00-18:00 in Intensive Care.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
The nursing and medical staff, the Matron, PALS.

Quiet time 12:00 to 13:00, 23:00 to 05:00
