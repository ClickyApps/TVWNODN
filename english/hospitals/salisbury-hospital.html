Interpreting services
The Customer Care Team can provide access to trained interpreters to support communication with patients who are non-English speakers, people for whom English is a second language, sign language, deaf, blind or partially sighted. The ward/department where the appointment is to take place will book the appointment in a timely and sensitive manner to enable the patient to be involved in decision making over his/her care. A 24-hour telephone interpreting service is also available for emergency situations or short non-complex appointments

By bus

From Salisbury City Centre Wilts & Dorset city service "Red 1" runs regularly from the city centre to the hospital. The bus leaves from Stop O in Blue Boar Row on the north side of the market square. The journey takes around 10 minutes. Buses run in an anti clockwise direction around the hospital site stopping in the following places:

  • The Green (for the Central and South parts of the hospital)
  • Springs Entrance (for Level 2 of the main hospital)
  • Main Entrance (for Levels 3 and 4 of the main hospital)

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
Is via intercome.
This is a locked down unit.

Talking to the medical team
The doctors are available to update you about your baby daily. The doctors do a daily ward round in te morning.

If you are not available at this time and would like to discuss your babies care, please speak to the nurse who will make an appointment for you to see the consultant

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurse supporting you to care for your baby will be able to update you on your babies care. If you need further information you cn speak to the Consultant or the manager of the unit.

Ward round
There is a daily ward round that happens in the morning, around 09.30 – 11.00.
On Wednesday there is a ward round including te lead consultant for Neonates. This normally takes place late morning.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Please speak to the nurse in charge, or one of the managers Jacquelyn Dalley or Charlotte Ashman-Scott.

Baby rest time
There is no specific 'Quiet/Rest' time. Quiet/rest time is tailored around both your baby and your needs.
