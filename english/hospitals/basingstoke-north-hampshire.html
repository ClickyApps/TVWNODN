Visiting information

Parents
Parents are welcome on the unit at any time and are encouraged to participate fully in their baby’s care. Grandparents of the baby are welcome between 2pm and 3pm and then between 6.30pm and 7.30pm. Under special circumstances other visitors may be allowed,but this would need to be discussed and agreed in advance with the Sister in charge.

Number of visitors
Due to space restrictions, we ask families to limit visiting to 3 at a cot at any one time. One of these should be a parent. Visitors can be rotated during the visiting hours.

Siblings
Siblings are welcome to visit with their parents. It is requested that all children are closely supervised.

Restrictions
All visiting is at the discretion of the Sister in charge. At busy times, numbers of visitors may need to be restricted and in the event of an emergency, visitors may be respectfully asked to leave.

Grandparents
Grandparents are very welcome to visit during visiting hours, 2pm to 3pm and also between 6.30pm and 7.30pm. They are asked to ring the bell and wait to be admitted.
We ask that grandparents visit only when accompanied by a parent.

Hygiene and infection prevention
Prevention of infection is treated with the upmost importance. We ask that parents and visitors gel their hands before entering the unit, then wash their hands with soap and water when inside the unit nursery.
Outdoor coats should be removed on entering the Neonatal Unit and hung on the hooks provided.

Family and friends
Although it is very natural for family and friends to wish to visit the Neonatal Unit, they are respectfully reminded that the babies are here because they are very small and/or sick and need to be in a quiet and controlled environment. For this reason we do not permit any visitors except parents, grandparents and the baby’s brothers and sisters.

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
We are very lucky to have a flat which we offer for parent to stay in overnight before they take their .baby home. Parents can use the kitchen facilities, and are welcome to make tea and coffee. There is a microwave and a small fridge for any food parents might like to bring. Menus are available from the postnatal ward for breastfeeding mums, and mums staying in the flat.

Parent facilities
The flat is also for the use of parents where they can enjoy a few minutes of “time out”, make a cup of tea or coffee and have a chat with other parents. There is a microwave if parents wish to bring meals from home to heat up. The sofas in the living room are very comfortable and there is also a TV.

Parent support group
To find out where the closest support groups to you please visit BLISS’s website (http://www.bliss.org.uk/support-in-your-area)
Further support can be obtained from organisations such as the National Childbirth Trust or specific organisations such as TAMBA (multiple births). Nursing staff will advise on appropriate support.

Facilities for siblings
The Neonatal Unit has a living room within the flat with a small number of toys for siblings. Children should not be left unsupervised in the flat and parents are respectfully asked to leave the room tidy. There is also colouring sheets and crayons within the unit to keep the little ones occupied.

Mobile phone use
Mobile phone, laptop and tablet use should be kept to an absolute minimum/emergency use when in the nurseries. Whilst in the nurseries, please ensure that your phone is set to “airplane mode” which will allow camera use. If a phone call must be taken, then this can be done in the flat.

Bereavement support
Losing a baby is an extremely traumatic event for everyone involved. Families will be given a private space and time to spend with their baby if this is desired. Nursing staff are trained to provide emotional support our lead nurse for bereavement is Deputy Sister Maureen Scullion.

Literature and contact information for useful organisations such as SANDS and the Child Bereavement Trust will also be provided.
http://www.childbereavementuk.org/

Counselling
Having a baby on the Neonatal Unit can be an exceptionally stressful time for families. Many parents find support by talking amongst themselves on the Unit and nursing staff are also always on hand to provide a listening ear. If any families are having particular difficulties, then staff would be happy to refer them to an appropriate source of support. This may be the PALS service, the chaplaincy or even for some practical advice on which benefits they may be entitled to and how to claim them.

The trust also has a Birth Reflections Team which provides primarily a listening service, offering a one to one appointment with a midwife within a quiet setting. The aim of the service is to enable you to gain some clarity for your personal enquiry regarding your birth experience. This may include re-visiting your birth story, enabling any ‘gaps’ in care to be addressed, acknowledging your perceptions, experience and feelings and preparing for a potential future pregnancy.

BLISS offer counsellor and trained advisor, please call freephone Helpline on 0500 618 140. The lines are open Monday to Friday, 9am to 9pm.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
Hampshire Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust are working towards BFI accreditation http://www.unicef.org.uk/BabyFriendly and have successfully achieved BFI Level 3. Support for breastfeeding is readily available on the Neonatal Unit and staff are proud of the number of mums who go home successfully breastfeeding their baby. Literature and contact numbers for other support groups are also widely available.

Breastfeeding support is given by all staff and there is a designated sister for feeding advice, Sister Liz Stanton. Mums are shown how to express their breast milk and to establish breast feeding when baby is ready to feed.

On the unit, privacy is protected by the use of screens and a separate room is available for expressing breast milk. Mums are encouraged to express milk at a very early stage.

When you have gone home there is also local support Basingstoke Maternity Unit have a clinic on Monday,Tuesday and Wednesday for babies up to 28 days old. Please ring 01256 313321 for an appointment.

Play specialists

Most babies are home with their families before play becomes appropriate, however, the play specialist who are based in the children’s unit are always happy to offer support to the older babies. Parents are encouraged to bring in mobiles, musical toys etc for stimulation.

Family centred care/developmental care lead
All staff are trained in the developmental care of preterm babies, which begins as soon as the baby is admitted with the use of incubator covers and positioning aids. Parents are shown how to “provide containment holding” and how to participate in their baby’s care. Further information regarding developmental care can be obtained from any of the nurses. Siblings are included where possible and made to feel part of the new extended family unit. Parents are welcome to be present during ward rounds to receive a full update on their baby’s progress and contribute to the care planning process. An individual appointment with their Consultant may also be arranged on request.

Nurse manager
The neonatal unit has two senior sisters Liz Stanton and Tania Nightingale who can be contacted to discuss any issues. Day to day there is always a nurse in charge on duty to talk through any worries parents may have. Grazia Sinar is the Clinical Services Lead for Neonatal Services and is always happy to talk to families.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The neonatal unit at North Hampshire Hospital is situated on the ground floor of the sherbourne building. In order to provide a safe and secure service, the neonatal unit has an intercom and a buzzer system to enter and leave the unit. The door will be released as soon as possible, please do not allow anyone else into the unit.

Talking to the medical team
There is a daily Consultant ward round , where you can speak to the Consultants, or ask the nursing staff to arrange a meeting with your babies Consultant at a mutually convenient time

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nursing staff are on hand 24-7 to discuss care. A member of nursing staff is allocated to each baby for every shift. Care can also be discussed with the Sister in charge of the unit. Medical staff are available as detailed above.

Ward round
A Ward round is conducted daily (including weekends) the time of the ward round varies, but the nurse looking after your baby will be able to tell you when it will be each day. Parents are encouraged to be present to receive the latest update on their baby and give them an opportunity to ask questions.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
If a family have a specific concern, they can talk to the nurse allocated to their baby. Alternatively, they might like to speak to the Sister in charge, one of the ward managers or the baby’s Consultant. If the family wish to speak to an independent person, then they would be advised to contact the Customer Care team (see the section entitled PALS) parents are strongly encouraged to express any concerns so that they can be addressed as promptly as possible.

Baby rest time
Quiet time is between 1-2pm (1300hrs-1400hrs). This is an hour each day that is designed to allow your baby a chance to completely rest and relax, with no interruptions. During this time blinds are shut, the lights turned down, and noise is kept to a minimum. The nurses will plan your babys cares so that where possible, they can be left undisturbed during this time.
