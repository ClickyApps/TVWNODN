Visiting information

Parents
Open visiting. However you will be asked to leave the unit during handovers/doctors rounds due to confidentiality when discussing other babies.

Number of visitors
HDU: max 2 per cot
Nursery: max 3 per cot.
May be less if the unit is very busy.

Siblings
Same as parents must be supervised at all times.

Restrictions
No other children can visit.
ALL VISITORS MUST BE FIT AND WELL

Grandparents
Tues – Thurs 18:00-19:00 and Sat/Sun 14:30-15:30
Must be accompanied by a parent
These visiting times are for visitors of choice by the parents, not just grandparents

Hygiene and infection prevention
Please remove outdoor coats/hats prior to entering the rooms.
Staff will explain about hand washing.

Family and friends
A questionnaire will be given to you prior to your discharge. Please complete it and put it in the sealed box by the nursery.

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
3 bedrooms are available for parents ‘rooming in’ prior to discharge

Parent facilities
A Sitting room with a TV and books and toys for siblings. There is also a toilet & shower and a kitchen with a fridge, microwave, kettle etc.

Parent support group
Facebook Page: MK NNU Special Babies

Facilities for siblings
Books, toys and TV available in the parent’s room.
Sibling packs provided as required.

Mobile phone use
Phones may be used on the unit, but we would ask that they are on silent whilst in the unit and calls should be taken outside the rooms. Chargers can only be plugged in in the parent’s room please.

Bereavement support
There is a nurse lead for Bereavement who would be available to discuss and support the families. There is also a bereavement midwife available.

Telephone and computer access
Not available on the unit.
Public phones in the main entrance and around the hospital.
Free Wi-Fi available

Counselling
For the future, we currently have 2 nurses in training

Breastfeeding facilities and support
Information and support for hand expressing, pump expressing and establishing breast feeding are available. Pumps and screens are provided on the unit.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
Please press the buzzer and wait for a member of staff to let you in. We may be busy so please be patient.

Talking to the medical team
You can be included in the discussions and plans for your baby on the ward round each day where you will be fully updated..

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
Each shift there will be a nurse looking after your baby who will introduce themselves.
On the doctors ward round you are welcome to be present and the doctors can discuss your babies care with you

Ward round
Approx. 10:00 daily
In addition there is a round on Friday afternoons at 15:00

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Nurse in charge of the shift or Karen Rice (Lead Nurse)

Baby rest time
Quiet time 12:30-14:30 daily
