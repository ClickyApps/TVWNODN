Cashpoint machine
There is no cashpoint on the Princess Anne Hospital site. The nearest machine is located in Southampton General Hospital and is situated through the main entrance, towards East Wing.

Religious and spiritual support
The chaplain is located on Level D, Centre Block at SGH. The chaplaincy/spiritual care teams facilitate and provide spiritual care for patients, carers and staff across the hospital. If the team itself does not contain a chaplain from a requested faith group, they will make every endeavour, through their various community contacts, to locate an appropriate person.

At Princess Anne Hospital, there is a quiet room on Amberwood Ward.

Catering/refreshment facilities
Zara's restaurant (E Level, PAH) is open for hot and cold snacks from 8:30 to 16:00 Monday to Friday (excluding bank holidays).

Spice of Life (B Level, SGH). The restaurant has a made-to-order-sandwich bar, a dedicated pizza and pasta bar, cooked-to-order meals and traditional favourites.

The opening hours are 7:30am until 19:30pm, Monday to Friday and 8:00 to 19:00 at weekends.

There are a number of vending machines dispensing hot drinks, snacks and ready to cook meals in both locations, available at all times.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
There are many ways of contacting PALS, you can either:

  • Speak to the local ward staff and ask to be put in touch with patient support services.
  • Call in to the information point just inside the main entrance of Southampton General Hospital.
  • Call 023 8120 6325
  • Email patientsupportservices@uhs.nhs.uk
  • Or write to Patient Support Services, C Level Centre Block, Mailpoint 81, Southampton General Hospital, Tremona Road, Southampton, SO16 6YD.

Interpreting services
Interpreter services are available via PALS or ask the nurse in charge of your baby.

Other shops on site/nearby
There are alternative food and shopping outlets at the main entrance of SGH (C level, Centre Block). These include WH Smith, The Stock Shop, The League of Friends and Costa. Tesco Express is located on the junction of Coxford Road and Lordswood Road, which is about 10 minutes walk from PAH.

Access to a midwife
Post natal care is available every day for mothers with a baby under 28 days old. Community midwives are also available.

Laundry facilities
The hospital is not able to wash patients’ personal clothing.
The hospital will supply a patient clothing bag that is safe to use in domestic washing machines.
This enables you to take soiled clothing home for laundering. The bag should be put unopened into the washing machine.
The nearest launderette ‘The Laundry Room’ is located at 367 Winchester Road (SO16 7DJ).
Please feel free to bring your own named bedding and clothes.

By car

Princess Anne Hospital has its own car park off Coxford Road with disabled and parent and-toddler bays; there is additional parking at Southampton General Hospital.

Subsidised parking is available for parents with a baby on the Neonatal unit. The cost of the parking is £2.00 per visit, but there is no time limit for each visit. This concession only applies to parents with a baby on the Neonatal unit, Princess Anne Hospital. Please take a ticket from the machine when you arrive at the car park. When you are ready to leave, hand your ticket to a member of staff who will validate it for you. The ticket then needs to be paid for at the pay point on level D or B, prior to leaving the car park.

Unsubsidised costs are:

  • Up to 1 hour: £2.00
  • Up to 2 Hours: £3.50
  • Up to 3 Hours: £4.20
  • Up to 4 Hours: £5.20
  • Up to 5 Hours: £6.20
  • Up to 6 Hours: £8.50
  • Between 6 and 12 hours: £11.10
  • Between 12 and 24 hours: £14.00
  • Long stay tickets are also available for 7, 14 or 30 days.

Further discounts are available for many patients and visitors, please ask the department or contact Travelwise on 02381 204133 or email Travelwise@uhs.nhs.uk

Neonatal unit facilities

Parent accommodation
Ronald McDonald House Southampton has now opened to provide free ‘home away from home’ accommodation to families with children in hospital.

With 53 en-suite bedrooms, the House is the biggest to date and is a facility where families can stay free of charge, for as long as their child is being treated as an in-patient of the hospital.

There is also limited accommodation for mothers on site within the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit to stay near their baby prior to discharge to establish feeding.

Parent facilities
The unit has a family lounge with sofas, TV’s and a children’s play area. There is kitchen area with fridges and microwaves for parents use. There are shower rooms and toilet facilities for parents on the unit. Resident breastfeeding mothers will receive on site catering.

Parent support group
The department has a parental support Website called Holding Little Hands http://www.holdinglittlehands.org/home.html . A parent’s Coffee Morning takes place the 4th Wednesday of each month 10:00-12:00 in Park Life Cafe (St James Park, SO15 5SD). On other Wednesdays we meet in the Neonatal Seminar Room on Broadlands Ward, level E PAH. This is a relaxed and friendly way for you to meet with other parents who are in a similar situation as yourself.

Facilities for siblings

Breastfeeding facilities and support
There is a dedicated expressing and breastfeeding support team on the unit who will meet with you within your first week. On admission you will be given all of the information you need for hand or pump expressing and will be given support throughout your stay.
There are screens and comfortable chairs available for expressing next to your baby alongside a newly refurbished expressing suite should you prefer to use this facility. We use microwaves to sterilise kits and for anything else you may need to sterilise for your baby.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The Neonatal Unit is accessed on D Level at PAH. At the D Level entrance, turn left through the double doors and past the lift lobby. The unit is located through the next set of double doors. Please press the buzzer on the door to gain access to the unit.

Talking to the medical team
Your baby will have a named consultant; meetings with medical staff are available. Please speak to the nurse looking after your baby.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurse looking after your baby will introduce themselves and will update you. Senior medical staff are available for consultations. You will be given a direct line phone number to the nursery where your baby is.

Ward round
Ward rounds take place in the morning. Doctors, nurses and the wider multidisciplinary team will review and make care plans for your baby. You are welcome to be present at the ward rounds.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
You can contact our Family Support Sister (Lisa Leppard) on 023 8120 8112, or contact our Matrons (Carol Woolridge and Fiona Lawson) on 023 8120 6001, or speak to the Patient Advice and Liaison Service on 023 8120 6325

Baby rest time
We endeavour to have several hours of quiet time during the afternoon with reduced light and activity in the nurseries, to enable babies to sleep. Many parents like to visit during this time, as it is calm and relaxed.
